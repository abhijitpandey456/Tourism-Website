Victoria Memorial, Kolkata

History

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. It was built between 1906 and 1921 to commemorate Queen Victoria's 25-year reign in India. Designed by Sir William Emerson in an architectural blend of British and Mughal styles, the memorial is now a museum and a major tourist attraction. It houses numerous artifacts from the colonial period and is surrounded by lush gardens.

Location

The Victoria Memorial is located near the Maidan area in central Kolkata.

Places to Visit Nearby

  • Indian Museum – One of the oldest museums in India
  • St. Paul's Cathedral – A beautiful Anglican cathedral near the memorial
  • Maidan – A large urban park ideal for relaxing walks
  • Birla Planetarium – Great for science and space enthusiasts
  • Park Street – Famous for restaurants and night life
