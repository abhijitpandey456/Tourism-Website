Dakshineswar Kali Temple, Kolkata

History

Dakshineswar Kali Temple is a famous Hindu temple located on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River in Kolkata, West Bengal. It was founded by Rani Rashmoni in 1855 and is dedicated to Goddess Kali. The temple is closely associated with the 19th-century mystic Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, who served as its head priest and attained spiritual enlightenment here. Built in traditional Bengali Navaratna architecture, the temple complex includes 12 Shiva temples and a temple dedicated to Radha-Krishna.

Location

The temple is located in Dakshineswar, about 20 km from central Kolkata.

Places to Visit Nearby

  • Belur Math – Headquarters of the Ramakrishna Mission, located across the river
  • Vivekananda Setu – A historic bridge offering river views
  • Adyapeath Temple – Another important Kali temple in the vicinity
  • Bally Bridge – Scenic and historic bridge ideal for photography
  • Ramakrishna Museum – Offers insights into Sri Ramakrishna and his teachings
