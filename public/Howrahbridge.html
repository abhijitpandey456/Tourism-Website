Howrah Bridge, Kolkata

History

Howrah Bridge, officially known as Rabindra Setu, is a cantilever bridge that spans the Hooghly River in West Bengal, India. Commissioned in 1943, it was constructed to replace a pontoon bridge and was designed by Rendel, Palmer & Tritton. It was built by Braithwaite, Burn & Jessop Construction Company. The bridge is a marvel of engineering, with no nuts and bolts used in its structure—it's entirely riveted. It carries daily traffic of over 100,000 vehicles and countless pedestrians, making it one of the busiest cantilever bridges in the world.

Location

Howrah Bridge connects the city of Kolkata with Howrah over the Hooghly River. It starts from Howrah Station and ends near the Burrabazar area in Kolkata.

Places to Visit Nearby

  • Howrah Railway Station – One of the busiest and oldest railway terminals in India.
  • Mullick Ghat Flower Market – A vibrant flower market near the base of the bridge.
  • Millennium Park – A riverside park ideal for family outings and photography.
  • Marble Palace – A beautiful 19th-century mansion with art collections.
  • Belur Math – Headquarters of the Ramakrishna Mission, located a short drive away.
