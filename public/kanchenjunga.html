Kanchenjunga Mountain

The Majestic Jewel of the Himalayas

History & Significance

Kanchenjunga is the third highest mountain in the world, standing at 8,586 meters (28,169 feet). It is located in the eastern Himalayas on the border between Nepal and Sikkim, India. The mountain is revered by the people of Sikkim and Nepal and is considered sacred. The name "Kanchenjunga" translates to "The Five Treasures of Snow", referring to its five distinct peaks.

First successfully climbed in 1955 by Joe Brown and George Band of a British expedition, Kanchenjunga has remained a symbol of grandeur and natural beauty. Local beliefs discourage climbers from stepping on the summit itself, out of respect for the mountain’s sanctity.

Location

The mountain is located in the eastern part of the Himalayas, straddling the border of eastern Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim.

Places to Visit Nearby

  • Yuksom: A historic town in Sikkim and base for many treks to Kanchenjunga.
  • Goechala Trek: A famous trekking route offering breathtaking views of the mountain.
  • Pelling: A scenic hill town with views of the Kanchenjunga range.
  • Tashiding Monastery: An ancient and spiritual Buddhist site nearby.
  • Darjeeling: A popular hill station offering distant but stunning views of Kanchenjunga.
